Chia Seed Pudding with Berries

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tsp honey (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup mixed berries

Instructions:

Combine all ingredients in a bowl, stir, and refrigerate for 4 hours or overnight. Top with berries before serving.

Why it's good for PCOD: Chia seeds are high in fiber and omega-3s, which help balance hormones and reduce inflammation.

Quinoa Salad with Avocado & Chickpeas

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup cooked chickpeas
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1/4 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 cucumber, diced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

Combine quinoa, chickpeas, avocado, tomatoes, and cucumber. Drizzle olive oil and lemon juice, toss to combine, and garnish with parsley.

Why it's good for PCOD: Quinoa helps with blood sugar control, and chickpeas provide fiber and protein for insulin balance.

Methi (Fenugreek) Paratha

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup fresh fenugreek leaves
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for kneading
  • Ghee or olive oil for cooking

Instructions:

Mix the flour, spices, and fenugreek leaves. Add water to form a dough, rest for 10 minutes, then roll into parathas and cook on a pan.

Why it's good for PCOD: Fenugreek helps manage insulin resistance, and whole wheat stabilizes blood sugar.
